Doctor's summary:- The document discusses salmonella, a type of bacteria that causes food-related illness in the United States. It is the most common cause of food poisoning, and its symptoms include diarrhea, fever, abdominal pain, and vomiting. The bacteria is commonly found in raw or undercooked meat, eggs, and dairy products, and can also be transmitted through pets or poor handwashing. Children and older adults are more likely to get sick from salmonella, and certain drugs, cancer treatments, inflammatory bowel disease, and international travel can increase the risk. Symptoms usually last a week or less, but in severe cases, it can be life-threatening. If symptoms persist for more than a week, or if a child, older adult, or person with a weakened immune system experiences severe symptoms, it is important to seek medical attention. Treatment for salmonella infection typically involves rehydration and antibiotics.
